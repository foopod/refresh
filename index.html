Hey, I'm Jono


What's a Jono?

I am a CodeClub mentor and an agile software developer at Vodafone. I studied software engineering at AUT, graduated 2 years ago and have been working at Vodafone since.

In my spare time I make video games, using a range of different technologies such as Android, iOS, Unity3D and HTML5. I am also part of the New Zealand Game Developers Association and an active member of the indie community.

Latest Projects

10 August LockedAway A text-based game that exploits reverse chronology.

This experience lets the player explore someones life by guessing the password to their online diary. The game was largely inspired by Christine Love's Even Cowgirls Bleed as well as more interactive fiction like Life is Strange.

It features reverse chronology, this sets it apart from a lot of stories, but it also made it a bitch to write.

Play It


8 July Sunset A HTML5 procedurally generated sunset scene.

This was my first time experimenting with Perlin noise and having another go making a HTML canvas based app.

Every time it loads almost everything changes, the hills, the sun and the sky gradient. The hills also have a sort of parallax effect as they scroll to the left as the sun sinks and the sky darkens.

Watch It


18 March Homage A mobile game that pays dedication to the classics.

Be the hero of the space galaxy. Manoeuvre your agile space ship around a dying star cruiser to save the people onboard by collecting their escape pods before they drift away into the emptiness of space.

This game I made for the sake of releasing a game. Up until this point I had never actually released anything. Long story short, this is a game that was made and released on the Google Play Store in roughly 8 hours, but then polished up in the following month.
